Biography

Howard is one of the earliest and most mysterious vendor figures in the Warehouse Wrecks universe. The only confirmed information about his past is that he came from China decades ago with deep printing knowledge. No reliable record explains who he was before that, where he learned his craft, or how he became connected to the warehouse.

Over time, Howard became tied to the label side of the operation. His world was made of rolls, printers, short labels, emergency orders, weekend crews, and the strange pressure of keeping production alive through paper, adhesive, ink, and promises.

In the archive, Howard is not portrayed as a normal vendor. He is closer to a warehouse myth: a quiet figure surrounded by label shortages, sudden problems, mysterious production runs, and unanswered questions. His presence becomes especially important in the songs Ho Lee Sheet and Thanks for Forgetting Me.

The Label Era

Howard’s era is remembered as a time when labels became more than simple components. They became a source of chaos, blame, pressure, and warehouse survival. When labels arrived short, wrong, delayed, or suspiciously handled, the entire operation felt the consequences.

His company, known in the lore as Abandoned Label Solutions, became a symbol of the unstable vendor world: modern printing machines, late-night work, rushed jobs, and the feeling that everything could collapse at any moment.

The song Ho Lee Sheet documents the beginning of Howard’s taking all over the warehouse, while Thanks for Forgetting Me expands the emotional side of broken promises.

Final Transmission

Howard’s disappearance is one of the most important unresolved events in the archive. After the collapse of his operation, he vanished from the known timeline.

His final known message was sent to Don:

“Hi Don, we close operations, thankya”

After that, no confirmed records explain where Howard went, what happened to the printers, or whether Abandoned Label Solutions truly ended — or simply disappeared into another warehouse myth.
