Biography

The I.T. Department is one of the most feared and least reachable entities in the Warehouse Wrecks universe. It is officially composed of Johana, Fabian, and Marcela, though the archive cannot confirm if all three are visible to regular warehouse personnel at the same time.

Their story is documented in Fix I.T. Yourself, a song that captures the ancient warehouse ritual of asking for help, being asked if a ticket was submitted, submitting the ticket, waiting, following up, waiting again, fixing the problem yourself, and then eventually being yelled by them and told the ticket was closed.

The department does not operate like a normal support team. It behaves more like a weather event. Sometimes it arrives. Sometimes it passes nearby. Sometimes it is mentioned in a meeting. Most of the time, the user is left staring at a printer that has chosen violence.

The Ticket Void

The Ticket Void is the central mystery of the I.T. Department. A request enters the system. A confirmation email appears. Hope briefly exists. Then the ticket begins its long spiritual journey into a place no warehouse worker has ever returned from.

Common ticket subjects include bugged scanners, printers refusing to print, the system freezing at the worst possible moment, network issues that only happen when you are busy, computers that suddenly forget how to be computers, and the classic emergency known as “I already restarted it three times.”

The official process is simple:

  1. Something breaks.
  2. User asks for help.
  3. I.T. asks, “Did you report it?”
  4. User reports it.
  5. Nothing happens.
  6. User fixes it manually.
  7. Ticket is closed for inactivity.

Archive Notes

The I.T. Department is classified as a collective character because its presence in the lore is larger than any single person. It represents the whole system: the ticket, the delay, the silence, the workaround, and the final email saying the issue has been resolved.

The archive does not confirm whether I.T. fixes problems or simply waits until the user adapts. More research is needed, but the research laptop is currently waiting for support.
